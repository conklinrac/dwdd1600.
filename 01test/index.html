Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital Citizenship is the idea of using technology and the internet responsibly, safely, and ethically. It's about how we behave online and how our actions affect ourselves and others in digital spaces.

What is digital law?

Digital law refers to the rules and laws that govern how people use technology, the internet, and digital content.

What is AUP?

An AUP is a set of rules that explain how technology, the internet, or devices are allowed to be used in a school, workplace, or organization.

What is plagiarism?

Plagiarism is when you use someone else's work, ideas, or words and present them as your own without giving proper credit.

Plagiarism = copying without credit.

What is academic dishonesty

Academic dishonesty is any action that gives an unfair advantage in schoolwork or misrepresents your learning.

Academic dishonesty = cheating or being dishonest in school.

What are the rules for AI generated content?

The rules for AI-generated content depend on the context (school, work, social media, or legal use), but there are some widely accepted principles.
